The World's Worst Weather

Date Tue 23 December 2014

The White Mountains have always been a refuge for me, a place to escape the stress and chaos of daily life and disappear for however long I choose. Sometimes it's a day hike up Welch and Dickey, Cannon, or the Franconia Ridge. Other times I find myself wandering the Pemi wilderness for days, enjoying the kind of sleep that only comes when laying out on a gravel bar under a cloudless, starry night sky. It's this solitude that brings me to these mountains, and this solitude, maybe, that has kept me from the summit building of Mount Washington for so long.



Winter finds this place an entirely different beast. The auto road is blown in deep with drifts, the cog sits dormant low on the east side, and the deck is devoid of the usual crowd. I'd almost say the place is quiet, serene, though the winds that howl outside the window say otherwise. My first night on the mountain has been nothing short of the expected chaos, as precipitation rapidly changes from snow to sleet to rain and the winds begin to build. I watched the sun set from my front steps last night, and I wonder when I will see it's bright rays again as we brace for the conditions forecast for the days ahead. As the wind lulls me to sleep, I find myself hoping the home of the 'world's worst weather' will live up to its name.
